## Sociology Journals

### American Sociological Review (ASR)
**Publisher**: American Sociological Association (ASA)
**ISSN**: 0003-1224 (print), 1939-8271 (online)
**Impact factor**: ~7 (2024)
**Submission fee**: None
**APC**: ~$2,600 for open access (optional)

**Manuscript requirements**:
- Text: ~12,000 words max (text + notes only; not references, tables, figures)
- Abstract: 150–200 words
- Format: 12pt font, double-spaced, 1-inch margins
- Line numbers required
- Tables: Word-formatted, end of document
- Figures: Separate files (EPS, TIFF, or PDF); 300+ DPI

**Submission system**: Editorial Manager (https://www.editorialmanager.com/asr)
**Blind review**: Yes (double-blind)
**Review time**: 3–6 months first decision
**Acceptance rate**: ~5–6%

**What ASR looks for**:
- Theoretical significance for sociology broadly
- Methodological rigor (increasingly causal)
- Original empirical contribution
- Clear hypotheses
- Explicit contribution statement

**Special issues**: ASR occasionally publishes research notes (~5,000 words) and symposia

**Citation format (ASA)**:
```
In-text: (Weber 1905) or Weber (1905)
Journal: Weber, Max. 1905. The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism. New York: Scribner.
Article: Smith, John. 2020. "Title of Article." American Sociological Review 85(3):400–420. https://doi.org/10.1177/...
```

### American Journal of Sociology (AJS)
**Publisher**: University of Chicago Press
**ISSN**: 0002-9602 (print), 1537-5390 (online)
**Impact factor**: ~6 (2024)

**Manuscript requirements**:
- No strict word limit; typical articles 8,000–15,000 words
- Abstract: 150 words
- Format: Double-spaced, 12pt, anonymous

**Submission system**: ScholarOne (https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ajs)
**Blind review**: Yes (double-blind)
**Review time**: 3–6 months
**Acceptance rate**: ~5–8%

**What AJS looks for**:
- Theoretical innovation and depth
- Historical, comparative, and cross-national work welcome
- More open to macro-structural arguments
- Strong theory section is especially valued

**AJS publishes**:
- Research articles
- Review essays (invited)
- Book reviews

**Citation format (Chicago Author-Date)**:
```
In-text: (Bourdieu 1984) or Bourdieu (1984)
Reference: Bourdieu, Pierre. 1984. Distinction: A Social Critique of the Judgement of Taste. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press.
Article: Smith, John. 2020. "Title." American Journal of Sociology 125(4):1234–1268.
```

### Demography
**Publisher**: Population Association of America (PAA) / Duke University Press
**ISSN**: 0070-3370 (print), 1533-7790 (online)
**Impact factor**: ~4.5 (2024)

**Manuscript requirements**:
- ~10,000 words max (text + notes)
- Abstract: ~150 words
- Keywords: 4–6

**Submission system**: Editorial Manager (https://www.editorialmanager.com/demography)
**Blind review**: Yes (double-blind)
**Review time**: 3–5 months
**Acceptance rate**: ~10–15%

**What Demography looks for**:
- Rigorous demographic methodology
- Clear demographic contribution (not just "we use demographic data")
- Population-level claims
- Detailed methods and robustness checks
- Online supplementary appendix common

**Also publishes**: Research Notes (shorter; ~4,000 words)

**Data deposit**: Required at ICPSR or equivalent

## Multidisciplinary / Interdisciplinary Journals

### Science Advances
**Publisher**: AAAS (American Association for the Advancement of Science)
**ISSN**: 2375-2548
**Impact factor**: ~13 (2024)
**Open access**: Yes (mandatory)
**APC**: ~$4,950 USD

**Scope for social science**: Broad; accepts computational social science, large-scale surveys, text analysis, network studies that have interdisciplinary significance

**Manuscript requirements**:
- Research articles: 4,000–6,000 words main text
- One-sentence abstract (for TOC/Twitter): ≤250 characters
- Full abstract: ~250 words
- Display items: Up to 7 (figures + tables combined)
- Methods: Can be placed after Discussion or in supplement

**Submission system**: AAAS portal (https://www.science.org/journal/sciadv)
**Review type**: Single-blind; editorial prescreening (~50% desk reject)
**Review time**: 6–10 weeks
**Acceptance rate**: ~30% of externally reviewed papers

**What Science Advances looks for**:
- Significant advance readable by scientists in other fields
- Large-scale data, novel methods, or major empirical scope
- Clear societal implications
- Rigorous statistics + code/data availability

### Nature Human Behaviour
**Publisher**: Nature Portfolio (Springer Nature)
**ISSN**: 2397-3374
**Impact factor**: ~29 (2024)
**Open access**: Optional (hybrid); ~€9,500 APC

**Scope**: Human behavior and society across disciplines (psychology, sociology, economics, neuroscience, public health)

**Manuscript requirements**:
- Article: 3,000–5,000 words
- Letters: ~1,500 words
- Abstract: ≤150 words (3 structured sentences: Background/Findings/Implications)
- Title: ≤90 characters
- Max references: 50
- Max figures + tables: 8 (or 6 for Articles)
- Extended Data: up to 10 items (supplement)

**Submission system**: https://mts.nature.com/cgi-bin/main.plex?form_type=display_auth_info&jrnl=nhb
**Review type**: Double-blind (since 2019)
**Review time**: 6–12 weeks
**Acceptance rate**: ~5% of submitted

**Required supplementary submissions**:
- Nature Research Reporting Summary (life sciences or social sciences version)
- Data availability statement
- Code availability statement
- Preregistration link (or statement of non-preregistration)
- Author contributions (CRediT taxonomy)

**What NHB looks for**:
- High impact; significance for human behavior broadly
- Rigorous methods; open science practices
- Broad interdisciplinary audience
- Translational / policy implications valued

### Nature Computational Science
**Publisher**: Nature Portfolio (Springer Nature)
**ISSN**: 2662-8457
**Impact factor**: ~26 (2024)
**Open access**: Optional (hybrid)

**Scope**: Development of computational methods and their application across natural and social sciences

**Manuscript requirements**: Same format as NHB

**Key difference**: The computational method itself is the primary contribution. Papers must:
- Develop or substantially improve a computational method
- Demonstrate application in a scientific domain

**Review time**: 8–12 weeks

### Social Forces
**Publisher**: Oxford University Press | **ISSN**: 0037-7732
**Impact factor**: ~5 (2024) | **APC**: optional open access

**Manuscript requirements**:
- ~12,000 words; Research Notes ~4,000 words
- ASA citation format; double-spaced, 12pt, line numbers
- Submission: https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/sf
- Blind: Double-blind | Turnaround: 3–5 months | Accept rate: ~10–12%

**Positioning**: Broad sociology; strong on race, immigration, stratification, organizations, family. More tolerant of exploratory work than ASR. Good for work that doesn't fit the narrow criteria of ASR (e.g., regional samples, methodological plurality).

**What Social Forces values**: Clear sociological contribution; good methods (not necessarily frontier ID strategy); policy-relevant work welcome.

## Resubmission Strategy

If rejected, use the following triage:
1. **Desk reject** (no review): Reframe for a different journal; address scope mismatch
2. **Rejection after review with detailed comments**: Major revision; address all reviewer concerns before next submission
3. **Revise and resubmit** at another journal after hard rejection: Write a detailed response memo addressing original reviewer concerns proactively

For comprehensive resubmission guidance (rejection triage, root cause diagnosis, journal ladder, intro reframe, cover letter): use `/scholar-respond resubmit`.

**Journal submission sequences** (by subfield):
- *Computational sociology*: NCS → Science Advances → NHB → PNAS → AJS → ASR
- *General sociology / stratification*: ASR → AJS → Social Forces → Social Problems
- *Demography*: Demography → PDR → JMF → Demographic Research
- *Sociology of culture*: Poetics → AJS → ASR → Cultural Sociology
- *Language and society*: Language in Society → Journal of Sociolinguistics → Applied Linguistics → AJS
- *Gender*: Gender & Society → ASR → Social Problems → Du Bois Review
- *Race and ethnicity*: ASR → Du Bois Review → AJS → Social Problems
- *Family*: JMF → Demography → PDR → Journal of Family Issues
- *Political sociology*: APSR → ASR → AJS → Politics & Society
- *Social movements*: Mobilization → ASR → AJS → American Behavioral Scientist
- *Methods*: SMR → Sociological Methods → NCS → Political Analysis

## Journal Selection Decision Guide

### By Paper Type

**Large-scale quantitative / causal identification study**

| Contribution type | Primary target | Secondary target |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Broad sociological significance + causal design | ASR | AJS, Social Forces |
| Population demography + decomposition | Demography | Population Studies, JMF |
| Computational + interdisciplinary (large data) | Science Advances | NHB, PNAS |
| New computational method | NCS | Science Advances |

**Theory-driven / interpretive / qualitative**

| Contribution type | Primary target | Secondary target |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Theoretical innovation | AJS | ASR, Sociological Theory |
| Ethnography / interview-based | AJS | ASR, Social Problems |
| Historical / comparative | AJS | ASR, Theory and Society |
| Language and society | Language in Society | J. Sociolinguistics, AJS |

**Mixed methods / policy-relevant**

| Contribution type | Primary target | Secondary target |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Policy-relevant with mixed methods | Science Advances | NHB, Social Forces |
| Education inequality | Sociology of Education | ASR, Demography |
| Immigration / race | ASR | AJS, International Migration Review |

### Key Distinguishing Questions

**Should this go to ASR or AJS?**
- ASR: Is the empirical contribution stronger than the theoretical one? Is it causal? Is it broadly sociological?
- AJS: Is the theoretical contribution the main contribution? Is it historically or comparatively grounded? Does it have a longer, richer argument?

**Should this go to a sociology journal or Nature/Science?**
- Sociology journal: Primary audience is sociologists; fine-grained discipline-specific contribution
- Nature/Science: Significant beyond sociology; large-scale data; interdisciplinary framing; accessible to non-sociologists; strong open science practices

**Should this go to NHB or NCS?**
- NHB: The finding is about human behavior; computational methods support the finding but are not the contribution
- NCS: The computational method is itself the contribution; involves advancing methodology

**Should this go to Demography or ASR?**
- Demography: Population process is the central focus; you care about rates, trends, cohorts, and demographic mechanisms
- ASR: The stratification or social mechanism is the focus; demographic data is the vehicle

### Red Flags for Each Journal

**Avoid submitting to ASR if**:
- Paper has no theoretical contribution beyond "we test X in a new context"
- Methods are not at current standards (e.g., no discussion of identification)
- Paper is already 15,000+ words

**Avoid submitting to NHB if**:
- No preregistration statement (will be flagged as major issue)
- Missing code/data availability statement
- Sample is purely WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) with no generalizability discussion
- Main text exceeds 5,000 words

**Avoid submitting to Demography if**:
- Paper doesn't center a demographic process (fertility, mortality, migration, family formation)
- Methods section is thin (Demography reviewers are technical)

**Avoid submitting to NCS if**:
- Code is not publicly available (non-negotiable)
- Computational method is not itself a contribution (using existing methods)
- Paper doesn't advance methodology
