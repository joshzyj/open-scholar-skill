## Round-Specific Openings

### R1 — Major Revision
> "We are grateful to the Editor and [two/three] reviewers for their careful reading of our manuscript, "[Paper Title]," and for the thoughtful and constructive feedback. The reviewers' comments have helped us improve the paper substantially. We have made significant revisions in response to all concerns, including [1-2 headline changes]."

### R1 — Minor Revision
> "We thank the Editor and reviewers for their positive assessment of our manuscript and for the helpful suggestions. We have addressed all remaining points as detailed below."

### R2 — Second Revision
> "We thank the Editor for the opportunity to revise further and the reviewers for their continued engagement with our work. We have carefully addressed each remaining concern. The changes in this round are targeted to the specific points raised and do not introduce new material."

### R3 — Third Revision
> "We appreciate the reviewers' and Editor's patience in guiding this manuscript to its final form. We have made the remaining [N] requested changes, which are detailed below."

### Response to Editor Only (Minor Points)
> "We thank the Editor for the constructive feedback. We have addressed all [N] points as detailed below."

## Phrase Bank by Situation

### Opening the Response to a Specific Comment

**Full agreement + action taken**:
- "We thank Reviewer [N] for this observation. We agree that..."
- "This is an important point. We have revised [section] to address it."
- "Reviewer [N] is correct that [issue]. We have [specific action]."

**Partial agreement**:
- "We appreciate this comment and agree that [aspect X needs clarification/revision]."
- "This comment points to a genuine ambiguity in our presentation."
- "We take this point seriously and have made the following changes, while also offering the following clarification..."

**Respectful disagreement**:
- "We appreciate Reviewer [N]'s concern and have given this careful consideration."
- "We respectfully maintain our original approach for the following reasons..."
- "While we understand the reviewer's concern about [X], we believe our approach is justified because..."
- "After reflection, we believe the current approach is most appropriate given [reason], though we have revised the text to clarify our rationale."

**Reviewer misunderstood the paper**:
- "We appreciate Reviewer [N]'s careful reading and believe this comment may reflect an ambiguity in our presentation."
- "To clarify: [explanation]. We have revised [location] to make this explicit."
- "We think Reviewer [N]'s concern stems from our underspecified description of [X]. We have now clarified this on p. [X]."

**Request is infeasible**:
- "We share Reviewer [N]'s interest in [goal]. Unfortunately, [reason why infeasible]. As a partial alternative, we [what you did instead]. We acknowledge this limitation in the Discussion (p. X)."
- "The data do not allow us to [request] because [reason]. We have added a note on this limitation on p. X."
- "The analysis suggested by Reviewer [N] would require [data/resources not available]. We have, however, conducted the closest feasible alternative..."

**Cross-reviewer overlap**:
- "Both Reviewers [N] and [M] raise concerns about [issue]. We agree this is a priority and have [action]."
- "This concern was raised by multiple reviewers, underscoring its importance. We have [action]."

**Addressing a new comment in R2+**:
- "We appreciate Reviewer [N]'s new observation regarding [topic]. We have [action]."
- "Reviewer [N] raises an additional point not addressed in the first round. We have [action]."

### Describing Specific Revisions

**Text added**:
- "We have added the following text to [section] (p. X, after para. Y): '[new text]'"
- "The [third/fourth] paragraph of the [Theory/Methods] section now reads: '[new text]'"

**Text revised**:
- "We have revised [sentence/paragraph] on p. X to now read: '[revised text]'"
- "We have rewritten the [fifth] paragraph of the Introduction to better convey [X]."

**Text deleted**:
- "We have removed [description] from [section] (formerly p. X) to [reason — reduce word count / eliminate redundancy / sharpen the argument]."

**Analysis added** (MANDATORY disk-citation format — every numeric claim must carry the `rr-results-registry.csv` row reference so reviewers and editors can audit the exact specification; numbers must match the CSV cell-for-cell):
- "We have run the [specification name] suggested by Reviewer [N]. The focal coefficient is β = [value], SE = [value], 95% CI = [lo, hi], p = [value], N = [value] [rr-results-registry.csv row=H_new model_id=[ID]]. Results are presented in [Table X / Appendix Table A1]."
- "We have added [a fixed-effects specification / AME / robustness check] as Table [X] in the Online Appendix. The main effect is AME = [value], 95% CI = [lo, hi], p = [value] [rr-results-registry.csv row=H_new model_id=[ID]]."
- Do NOT paraphrase the Task agent's prose summary; always cite the CSV row. Step 3b verify-numerics enforces equivalence.

**New citation added**:
- "We have added citations to [Author Year] and [Author Year] in the [Literature Review] section."
- "Following Reviewer [N]'s suggestion, we have incorporated [Author Year]'s work on [topic] in the Theory section (p. X)."

**Figure / table modified**:
- "We have revised Figure [X] to [add error bars / add individual data points / improve labeling]."
- "Table [X] now reports [AMEs / additional statistics / new model specification]."

## Response Length Guidelines

| Comment type | Recommended response length |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Minor typo / citation fix | 1–2 sentences + note of revision |
| Clarification of existing text | 2–4 sentences + quote revised text |
| Request for new analysis | 3–6 sentences explaining the analysis + describe results |
| Major theoretical concern | 5–10 sentences defending or revising position |
| Infeasible request | 4–6 sentences explaining why + describe what you did instead |
| Reviewer misunderstood | 3–5 sentences clarifying + show revised text |
| Cross-reviewer overlap | 5–8 sentences noting overlap + comprehensive response |

### Response Length by Round

| Round | Average response per comment | Total letter length |
|-------|-----------------------------|--------------------|
| R1 Major | 3–8 sentences | 5–15 pages |
| R1 Minor | 2–4 sentences | 2–5 pages |
| R2 | 2–5 sentences | 2–6 pages |
| R3 | 1–3 sentences | 1–2 pages |

```

[xxx]), submitted in response to the Major Revision decision of [date].

We are grateful to the Editor and [N] reviewers for their constructive
and detailed feedback. We have carefully revised the manuscript to
address all concerns raised. The major changes include:

1. [Major change 1 — briefly, 1 sentence]
2. [Major change 2]
3. [Major change 3]

[Optional: 1–2 sentences addressing the editor's specific priority if
one was identified in the decision letter.]

separately. All changes in the manuscript are marked in [blue text /
tracked changes].

The revised manuscript is [N] words, within the journal's [N]-word

address all reviewer concerns.

Sincerely,
[Name, Title, Affiliation, Email]

```

Please find enclosed our manuscript, "[Title]," for consideration for
publication in [New Journal Name].

[1–2 sentences on why this paper is a strong fit for this journal's

not been published elsewhere, and is not under review at another journal.

## Editorial Response Template

When the editor provides separate comments (distinct from reviewer comments):

```
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

Editor Comment 1:
"[Exact text of editor's comment]"

Response:
We thank the Editor for [this important observation / this helpful

```

## Tracked Changes Annotation System

When producing revision diffs, use these markers consistently:

| Marker | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `[ADDED: R#.#]` | New text added to address reviewer comment |
| `[REVISED: R#.#]` | Existing text revised to address reviewer comment |
| `[DELETED: R#.#]` | Text removed per reviewer request |
| `[MOVED: R#.#]` | Text relocated from one section to another |
| `[CONSIST.]` | Change made for internal consistency (not reviewer-requested) |

Example:
> "We employ a difference-in-differences design [REVISED: R1.1] with an event study specification to estimate the causal effect of [treatment] on [outcome]. [ADDED: R1.1] We present pre-trend tests in Appendix Figure A1, which show no differential trends in the pre-treatment period (F = 1.23, p = .29)."

## Tracking Changes in the Manuscript

**Best practices**:
1. Use a distinct color (blue is conventional) for all new or revised text
2. Strikethrough deleted text (or use Word Track Changes)
3. In the response letter, reference page numbers from the revised (not original) manuscript
4. For major restructuring, include a note: "Note: page numbers in this response refer to the revised manuscript."

**For Nature journals**: Some journals request a separate "clean" and "tracked changes" version — check the journal's revision guidelines.

**For R2+**: Only mark changes from this round, not cumulative changes from all rounds.

## Tone Calibration Guide

**Too defensive** (avoid):
> "Reviewer 2 is simply wrong about this. Our analysis is correct because..."

**Too deferential** (avoid):
> "We humbly apologize for this egregious error and have completely rewritten this section..."

**Just right**:
> "We thank Reviewer 2 for raising this concern. We believe the discrepancy arises from [explanation]. Nevertheless, we have revised the text to clarify [X], and have also run [analysis] to address the underlying concern (see Table A2)."

**When you cannot do what they asked**:
> "We appreciate this suggestion and have seriously considered it. The [analysis/data/approach] is not feasible in the current study because [specific reason]. We have, however, [best alternative you could do]. We have also added a sentence acknowledging this limitation in the Discussion (p. X, para. 3)."

**When you firmly disagree**:
> "We have carefully considered this comment and respectfully maintain our original position. [Three specific reasons: logic, prior literature, or your own data.] We have revised p. X to articulate this reasoning more explicitly, so readers can evaluate it themselves."

**When a reviewer is hostile or unfair**:
> "We appreciate Reviewer [N]'s engagement with our work. We respectfully note that [the characterization of our argument / the description of our method] may not fully capture our approach. To clarify: [factual correction]. We have revised p. X to make this point clearer."

**When acknowledging a genuine mistake**:
> "We thank Reviewer [N] for catching this error. [Describe the error briefly.] We have corrected [the calculation / the table / the text] on p. X. This correction [does / does not] affect our substantive findings."
